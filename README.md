# Ultrashort-pulse laser microtexturing of printed circuit board copper pads to reduce voids in VQFN solder joints

This repository contains the X-ray image data and void-area analysis results associated with the study:

**Ultrashort-pulse laser microtexturing of printed circuit board copper pads to reduce voids in VQFN solder joints**

Authors:  
Yukihisa Otani, Takaya Ozaki, Tatsuo Hasegawa, and Tatsuo Shimizu

This study experimentally evaluated ultrashort-pulse laser groove microtexturing as a method for reducing solder voids in very-thin quad flat no-lead (VQFN) solder joints on copper pads of FR-4 printed circuit boards. Laser texturing conditions, including processed area, hatching pattern, and pitch, were systematically varied. After texturing, VQFN components were mounted by reflow soldering, and the void ratio of the central thermal pad was quantified using X-ray imaging.
